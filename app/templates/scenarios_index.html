What is a prediction?

A prediction is a single claim about the market that can take one of a fixed set of states — e.g. "do buyers prefer self-serve or sales-led?". Each piece of evidence we tag nudges the prediction's posterior toward one state. Predictions are the atoms scenarios are built from.

What is evidence?

Evidence is a single finding tagged against a prediction — direction (support / contradict / neutral), strength, and a credibility weight. Tagged on the Stream, proposed by the classifier sweep or added manually. Each row's contribution decays over time and feeds the prediction posterior.

What is a scenario?

A scenario is a future state of the market defined as a weighted bundle of predictions each pinned to a required state — e.g. "market consolidates around two sales-led incumbents". Its probability is the weighted product of its predictions' current posteriors (independence assumed). Use scenarios to track where the weight of evidence is pointing across competitors.

What lives in settings?

The knobs that drive the Bayesian math: likelihood ratios (how strongly each direction × strength bucket moves a state), source credibility, decay half-life (how fast old evidence fades), priors, and scenario weights.
