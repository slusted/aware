What is a predicate?

A predicate is a single claim about the market that can take one of a fixed set of states — e.g. "do buyers prefer self-serve or sales-led?". Each piece of evidence we tag nudges the predicate's posterior toward one state. Predicates are the atoms scenarios are built from.

What is evidence?

Evidence is a single finding tagged against a predicate — direction (support / contradict / neutral), strength, and a credibility weight. Tagged on the Stream, proposed by the classifier sweep or added manually. Each row's contribution decays over time and feeds the predicate posterior.

What is a scenario?

A scenario is a future state of the market defined as a weighted bundle of predicates each pinned to a required state — e.g. "market consolidates around two sales-led incumbents". Its probability is the weighted product of its predicates' current posteriors (independence assumed). Use scenarios to track where the weight of evidence is pointing across competitors.

What lives in settings?

The knobs that drive the Bayesian math: likelihood ratios (how strongly each direction × strength bucket moves a state), source credibility, decay half-life (how fast old evidence fades), priors, and scenario weights. Editable controls land in Spec 05; for now these live in the DB and are tuned via SQL — see docs/scenarios/01-foundation.md §Configurability surface.
